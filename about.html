About

Value Statement

Fundamentally, my work is about blending analysis and egalitarian values in order to understand public problems, and offer powerful solutions. In practice, this centers on rigorous, reproducible analyses, which made accessible to key stakeholders and the public alike serve as a critical precursor to decision making. Such commitment to policy research and novel knowledge creation is built upon my will to build a more just society conducive to well-being for all. My pursuit of these ends is a process guided chiefly by the following values:

  • Empathy

  • Empiricism

  • Community

  • Sustainability

About Me

I am a researcher trained in policy analysis focused on the intersection of data, public health and social equity. I have recently completed a Master of Public Policy at the University of Illinois Chicago, specializing in public health policy, where I maintained a GPA of 3.92 and have sharpened skills in the domains of communication and analysis. Prior to my graduate studies, I obtained a Bachelor of Arts in Political Science from the University of Illinois Urbana‑Champaign with a concentration in public policy and democratic institutions.

Professional Experience

I work as a research assistant at the Nathalie P. Voorhees Center for Neighborhood and Community Improvement. There, my main project is ADA‑PARC—a national research effort that examines quality of life disparities for people with disabilities organized into three main categories: Community Living, Community Participation, Work Economic. My work within this project has involved building a complex U.S. Census data pipeline, a website to display results using R-Shiny, developing interactive visualization dashboards using Tableau, and producing an accessible report using Quarto/LaTex. While at the Voorhees Center I have also contributed to the City of Chicago’s Point-In-Time Homeless Count (PIT). Prior to this, I served as a research intern at the Jefferson Educational Society in Erie, Pennsylvania, where I produced publications and assisted with research projects, including an analysis fo the human rights abuses prior to the 2022 FIFA World Cup in Qatar.

Projects & Interests

My portfolio reflects interests in public health, socioeconomic equality and program evaluation, but my interests are numerous. Recent projects include modeling the correlates of mood disorders and predictors of chronic disease using CDC data, analyzing rent burden across the Chicago metropolitan area, examining support for wealth redistribution using the GSS, and evaluating the potential impact of the Special Supplemental Nutrition Program for Women, Infants and Children (WIC) using synthetic data.

Skills & Leadership

I bring a combination of policy analysis, quantitative and qualitative research methods, econometrics, and strong written and verbal communication skills. My technical toolkit is most developed within R-Studio/Quarto, Git Hub, Tableau, and Shiny for modeling, visualization and reproducible reporting. Beyond research, I have experience leading and organizing mental health advocacy efforts as a former president of Active Minds at the University of Illinois Urbana‑Champaign.

Through this portfolio I aim to share a glimpse into my technical abilities and soft skills which make me an asset to any organization interested in problem solving. Thank you for being here, and feel free to explore my projects and reach out via email if you would like to connect.
